
The Modernization Imperative: Shifting left is for suckers. Shift down instead
post: https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/application-development/richard-seroter-on-shifting-down-vs-shifting-left/

- trend to optimize resources - 'reducing cost'
- example self-check cashier in supermarket - no cashier people - customer can do by him/her-self
- analogy to full stack engineer - push to wider overview without a focused expertize to shine
- 'shifting left' - idea of the practice of incorporating security and QA reviews earlier in the development process
                  - burden on devs to know multiple tools - as regular job offer - frontend, backend, DB, queues, devops, security, blah blah
- 'shift down' - compress the tech-stack, leverage platform abstraction - delegate to platform engineering teams (cough-cough, google)
- platform teams - allows you to use the platform as product for your module/project
                 - reduces cognitive load (all handled by them) and support innovations in your project

Shifting Left Vs Shifting Down - Mesoform
post: https://www.mesoform.com/resources/blog/review/shifting-left-vs-shifting-down

shift left
- usual, sequential development
- catch problems early
   - unit test,
   - CI/CD,
   - code review,
   - code guide
   - perf monitoring
   - QA iteration
- usually dev needs bigger set of knowledge (frontend, backend, framework, DB, containers, build systems, etc.)
- fail fast principle, minimize future problems
- brings:
   - cognitive load on dev - loner to integrate a new one
   - more tools to learn - monitoring, frameworks, collaboration between departments
   - resistance to changes
   - need to scale and complexity

shift down
- focusing on domain experts
- introduces platform team
   - maintain infrastructure, tools and services
   - platform abstraction that other teams are focused on its domain
   - minimizes group of technology for other teams to master
   - chooses 'opinionated' framework with default config, monitoring service, dashboard templates, etc. that other team can simply use
   - maximizes security hidden under abstraction, introduces security policy & tools into workflow
- reduces cognitive load on teams using platform abstraction
- teams can focus on its domain - consistency, probably increased productivity, innovation (knowing the domain)
- need to balance abstraction, selecting right tools for platform and customization, investment into platform team (time and money)

real world
- mostly mixed approach - finding balance -> depends on project





Shift Down to the Platform, not Left to the Dev - Michel Murabito
post: https://mia-platform.eu/blog/shift-down-to-the-platform/

shift left
- as mentioned before - finding problems in early stages (with complexity the most reason to delay release)
- might not be enough when deployed
   - secure only for current development, not already running app (maybe old dependency, API flaws, etc.)
   - https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbestechcouncil/2022/08/29/the-security-holes-of-shift-left
- more detailed steps in the article with graphic

shift right
- kinda test in prod, wanna see real user interacting with the app
- example A/B testing
- helps catching real usage problems (e.g. bugs, security, etc), needs proper monitoring

shift down
- platform team for platform abstraction
- dev can focus on its domain while using platform as product - faster integration, smaller cognitive load (in theory) - less stuff to learn
- teams loses more control for platform team - needed collaboration between teams and platform team - feedback, best practices, ...





How shift-left development practices can be successfully applied to your customer services and support organization
post: https://kepner-tregoe.com/resources/whitepapers/shift-left-no-shift-down-for-services-support-success/

- research from the IBM System Science Institute found that if issues are identified early in development,
  they cost approximately $80 to fix. But the same problems cost $8,000 to fix if detected during production — 100 times as much
- longer article with similar message as before but for support service rather then devs :)
   - idea of shift down is to pass customer to the more experienced tech support only if lower skill will not manage
   - experienced staff can be focused on more important stuff
